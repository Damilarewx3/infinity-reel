How We Shot the Epic Car Chase Scene in Our Latest Film

By Gary Gordon | January 1, 2024

Car chase scenes are a staple of action films, but shooting them is no easy feat. Here’s how we pulled off the thrilling chase sequence in our latest project, "The Last Reel".

Pre-Production: Planning the Chase

Before we even picked up a camera, we spent weeks planning the car chase. We worked with our stunt coordinator to map out the sequence, ensuring it was both exciting and safe. Storyboards were created to visualize each shot, and we scouted multiple locations to find the perfect setting.

Key decisions included:

  • Choosing the right vehicles for the chase.
  • Planning the route to minimize risks.
  • Coordinating with local authorities for permits and road closures.

On Set: Coordinating Stunts and Camera Work

On the day of the shoot, our team was divided into several units: the stunt team, camera operators, and safety personnel. We used a combination of practical effects and precision driving to create the high-speed action.

Some of the techniques we used included:

  • Mounting cameras on the cars for dynamic angles.
  • Using drones for aerial shots.
  • Filming at different times of the day to capture the perfect lighting.

Safety was our top priority, and we had paramedics and fire crews on standby throughout the shoot.

Post-Production: Adding Sound and Visual Effects

Once the footage was in the can, our post-production team took over. They enhanced the visuals with CGI to add debris, sparks, and other effects. The sound design team layered engine roars, tire screeches, and explosions to make the scene feel immersive.

Key post-production steps included:

  • Editing the sequence for pacing and tension.
  • Color grading to match the film’s aesthetic.
  • Adding a custom score to heighten the excitement.

Conclusion

Shooting a car chase scene is a massive undertaking, but the result is always worth it. We’re incredibly proud of how this sequence turned out, and we can’t wait for you to see it in "The Last Reel".
